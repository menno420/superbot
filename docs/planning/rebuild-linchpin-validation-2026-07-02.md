# Rebuild linchpin validation — the owner-gate evidence package

> **Status:** `reference` (2026-07-02, evidence package) — the empirical de-risking of the two unproven linchpins the
> [Phase-2 design spec](rebuild-design-spec-2026-07-02.md) rests on, produced BEFORE the Phase-3
> owner gate so the gate call is informed by measurement, not assertion. Both artifacts are built,
> runnable, and tested in this repo: the **golden behavioral harness** ([`parity/`](../../parity/README.md),
> coverage in [`parity/COVERAGE.md`](../../parity/COVERAGE.md)) and the **grammar-expressiveness
> spike** ([`tools/grammar_spike/`](../../tools/grammar_spike/__init__.py), unit ledger in
> [`RESULTS.md`](../../tools/grammar_spike/RESULTS.md)). No `disbot/` behavior changed; no new-repo
> (`sb/`) code exists; every §5.3 compat freeze is untouched.
>
> **The one-line verdict: PROCEED, with six named grammar amendments folded into the design spec
> before K2 — the evidence says the design's two scariest assumptions hold, one of them only after
> the amendments this spike surfaced.**

---

## 0. What was at stake

An independent review of the (otherwise approval-quality) design spec found two linchpins asserted
but unproven:

1. **The golden harness IS the entire safety model** (§6, §10.1 risk 1). "Red-until-parity" is only
   as trustworthy as the fraction of real behavior the harness captures — and it had never been
   built, so that fraction was unknown. It is the critical path: it can only be captured while the
   old bot is live, and every port band leans on it.
2. **Grammar expressiveness was asserted, not measured** (§2, §10.1 risk 5). The "no hand-written
   views layer" thesis holds only if ~80% of real surfaces fit the manifest grammar as tier-1/2
   declarations rather than tier-3 escape-hatch code. Nobody had expressed a real subsystem in the
   grammar.

Both were buildable now, without the owner gate, because they touch no new-repo production code.
They are now built. This document reports what the building measured, what it broke in the design,
and the resulting go/no-go recommendation.

## 1. Linchpin A — the golden harness, built and measured

### 1.1 What got built

`parity/` — the full Phase-0.5 deliverable ([README](../../parity/README.md) is the operating
manual):

- **A max-fidelity in-process driver.** The real `bot1.bot` object (so the production
  `before_invoke` governance gate, the `on_command_error` typo re-dispatch, `on_interaction`
  router, and metrics listeners are live), booted through the behavior-relevant subset of
  `bot1.main()`, driven by synthetic gateway payloads through the **real discord.py
  `ConnectionState` parser** — real guilds/members/messages, real converters, real cooldown
  buckets, real view store. One fake seam: the HTTP boundary (`fake_http.py`), where every
  outbound effect is captured — and an **unknown outbound call fails the capture loudly** rather
  than silently dropping behavior (the capture-integrity guard; it caught three real gaps during
  development).
- **Four observation channels per case:** outbound calls (messages/embeds/components with
  custom_ids, edits, deletes, role/channel mutations), bus events (a tap subscribed to all 47
  `KNOWN_EVENTS`), the full-database row-level delta, and (for clicks) the resolved component.
- **A determinism layer that is proven, not hoped for** — `capture` → `check` replays
  byte-identical (proven at curated scale, then at full-corpus scale; every determinism rule
  exists because a replay diff caught its class — logical-clock snowflakes, `time.time` pinning,
  spawned-task attribution, `delete_after` collapse, uuid/auto-custom_id symbolization,
  wall-clock view timeouts, position-independent per-case clock bases, and process-mutating
  command exclusion).
- **Two corpus populations:** 11 curated multi-step flows (karma grant/cooldown/self-error,
  economy daily, logging enable→bind, settings hub, help panel, a blackjack round with a real
  button click through the view store, moderation warn, XP chat award) + a **mechanical breadth
  sweep** over every enumerable prefix/slash command with synthesized arguments — usage-error
  replies are captured too (an error embed is behavior).
- **The replay CLI** (`python3.10 -m parity.run check`) — the red-until-parity primitive the new
  repo will consume, and a current-bot regression net today.

### 1.2 The coverage number — the safety ceiling, measured

*(Generated by `python3.10 -m parity.run coverage`; full tables + the named uncovered tail in
[`parity/COVERAGE.md`](../../parity/COVERAGE.md). Denominators come from the booted bot's own
registries — never hand-maintained lists.)*

**Corpus: 465 golden cases** (460 gating + 5 named advisory). Headline numbers (2026-07-02 final capture):

| Surface | Captured | Total | Coverage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prefix commands | 390 | 406 | **96%** |
| Slash commands | 64 | 73 | **88%** |
| Persistent-panel components (custom_ids) | 60 | 64 | **94%** |
| Persistent panels (>=1 component captured) | 9 | 11 | **82%** |
| Bus events observed | 10 | 47 | **21%** |
| DB tables touched by >=1 golden | 26 | 105 | **25%** |
| Settings keys mutated by >=1 golden | 3 | 120 | **2%** |

**The two-line honest reading:** the *invocation surface* (what users can trigger) is essentially
fully captured — 96%/88%/94% with every exclusion named and reasoned (process-lifecycle ops,
host-state diagnostics, and the determinism-seam class below account for the gap). The *deep-state surface*
(which events fire, which tables move, which settings mutate) is deliberately thin at 21%/25%/2% —
those dimensions grow with curated multi-step flows (config edits, game completions, event-rich
paths), which is exactly the per-band work the port loop schedules; the numbers make the remaining
work measurable instead of invisible. **Replay stability is proven, not assumed: the final gating
replay is 459/459 green**, with a 5-case named advisory tail (the xfail pattern — settle-budget
racers kept for inspection, non-gating). The safety ceiling is no longer unknown: it is ~96% broad,
replay-stable, and measurably shallow, with the deepening mechanism in place.

**How to read it honestly:**

- Coverage is **breadth** (≥1 captured invocation per surface), not path depth. Depth lands
  per-subsystem at each `pending → ported` flip via the spec's required coverage notes (§10.1
  mitigation 1), for which the harness now provides the mechanism.
- The uncovered tail is **named, with reasons**, in COVERAGE.md — nothing is silently out of
  scope. The structural gaps (scheduled-loop behavior, wait_for-driven wizards, voice) are listed
  in the README's deviations ledger.
- **Usage-weighted coverage is not claimable yet:** the only usage signal
  (`command_total{cog,command,result}`, `services/metrics.py:84`) is an in-process Prometheus
  counter with no historical scrape in this environment. The Phase-0.5 telemetry sidecar
  (§2.10.4) must scrape production before "X% of real *traffic*" can be stated. Flagged, not
  faked.

### 1.3 What building it proved / disproved about the design

- **Proved: the harness architecture the spec sketched is viable without new dependencies.**
  dpytest/testcontainers (named in §6) turned out unnecessary AND unavailable-shaped for this
  repo: dpytest pins against discord.py versions (we run 2.7.1) and testcontainers needs Docker
  the sandbox lacks. The shipped pattern — fake HTTP over the real state machine + a
  `DATABASE_URL` Postgres — is stronger (real dispatch, real converters) and CI-compatible.
  **Design-spec amendment (soft):** §6 should name this driver pattern rather than "dpytest or
  equivalent."
- **Proved: "command-in → embed/DB-out" under-describes what is capturable.** Events and
  component trees come free and materially widen the oracle (the §5.3 event-payload freeze is
  directly checkable from goldens: every observed event's kwargs are recorded).
- **Disproved: "reuse the `evals/` corpus" as a harness input.** `tests/evals/` drives the
  `AIGateway` (model-answer quality), not commands — the two oracles compose (this harness owns
  the deterministic surface; evals own the AI-answer surface) but share no cases. The spec's §6
  phrasing should say "the evals corpus remains the AI-surface oracle" instead of implying reuse.
- **Found: a new kernel design requirement — injectable clock + RNG.** A handful of current-bot
  paths are nondeterministic BY CONSTRUCTION (unseeded private `random.Random` instances in spawn
  selection; real-TTL in-memory caches keyed on wall time; `datetime.now()` composed into ids) —
  no harness can pin them from outside, so their commands are excluded-with-reason. The rebuild
  kernel should make time and randomness injectable services (the engines already centralize the
  choreography, so this is cheap at K5/K7) — then *every* surface is golden-testable by
  construction. This requirement exists in no earlier rebuild document; the harness discovered it.
- **Measured: determinism is the real engineering cost.** Eight distinct nondeterminism classes
  had to be pinned before full-corpus replay went green (see README § determinism model — each
  entry names the replay diff that caught it). The new repo inherits these for free via this
  harness, but any *additional* capture channel (e.g. scheduled loops) will pay the same class of
  cost — budget it in K10.

## 2. Linchpin B — the grammar, exercised against reality

### 2.1 What got built

`tools/grammar_spike/` — an explicitly-labeled spike (NOT the kernel; `sb/` stays behind the
gate): a faithful prototype of every §2 spec dataclass with S/A/O field-role metadata and the
§3.2-phase-1 validators, plus **three complete, source-cited real manifests** spanning the
difficulty range —

| Manifest | Difficulty class | Real surface expressed |
|---|---|---|
| [`karma.py`](../../tools/grammar_spike/manifests/karma.py) | simple domain | 4 commands, 4 settings, react-to-thank listener, `karma.granted` event, 2 INV-K stores, card panel, leaderboard |
| [`server_logging.py`](../../tools/grammar_spike/manifests/server_logging.py) | operator config (richest lane surface) | 6 commands, 8-button panel (verbatim `logging_panel.*` ids), 12 settings incl. the safe-default-ON showcase, 11 bindings + 11 resources, 3 bus subscriptions, 8 gateway listeners |
| [`blackjack.py`](../../tools/grammar_spike/manifests/blackjack.py) | stateful game (escape-hatch-heavy) | 4 commands, board + result renderers, 3 game moves, 3 `ChallengeSessionSpec` lifecycles with escrow/settle-once, reaction-join, decision-10 leaderboard |

— and a **measurement ledger** (`measure.py`): every real surface unit of those subsystems (95
units, enumerated from source, not from the manifests), each classified tier-1/2/3 twice — under
the spec **as written** and with this spike's **proposed families** — with a per-unit rationale a
reviewer can dispute line-by-line. The spike is structured to seed K1/K2 if the gate opens
(`spec.py` → `sb/spec/`; the manifests → their port bands' declarations).

### 2.2 The number — does the ~80% hypothesis hold?

From [`RESULTS.md`](../../tools/grammar_spike/RESULTS.md) (regenerate:
`python3.10 -m tools.grammar_spike.measure`):

| Scope | Units | Tier-1/2 fit — spec as written | Tier-1/2 fit — with proposed families |
|---|---|---|---|
| karma | 15 | 80% | 87% |
| logging | 62 | 79% | **97%** |
| blackjack | 18 | 44% | 44% |
| **OVERALL** | **95** | **73%** | **85%** |

**Verdict: the hypothesis holds — but only with the grammar amendments below.** As written, the
grammar lands at 73% on this spanning sample, under the 80% bar, because one whole primitive
family is missing (G-1). With the five proposed tier-2 families it clears the bar at 85%, and the
shape is exactly what §10.1 predicted: config/operator surfaces are nearly pure declaration
(97% for logging — the generated-panel payoff is real), while stateful games sit at ~44% *by
design* — their renderers and moves are §2.9's named escape-hatch classes, and the session
*choreography* (escrow, settle-once, timeouts, recovery) does leave the domain via
`ChallengeSessionSpec`. Sample caveat: three subsystems, escape-hatch-heaviest class included;
most of the 43 shipped subsystems are karma/logging-shaped, so 85% is more likely a floor than a
ceiling for the full port — but that extrapolation is a judgment, marked as such.

### 2.3 The grammar amendments the spike forces (fold into the spec before K2)

1. **G-1 — `GatewayListenerSpec` (the load-bearing one).** §2 has bus events only; raw Discord
   gateway listeners have NO primitive — and the operator band lives on them (server logging alone
   consumes 8: `logging_cog.py:170–311`; karma's react-to-thank `karma_cog.py:95`; blackjack's
   reaction-join `:410`). Without it, every gateway-driven feature is tier-3 by definition and the
   wiring map goes blind exactly where the current repo's is. Proposed: `gateway_event` +
   `handler` ref + a declarative `gate` predicate the kernel checks before dispatch (the shipped
   fast-gate pattern). This single family moves logging 79% → 90%+.
2. **G-2 — list-valued settings.** The shipped exclusion lists (`logging_ignored_channels/users`,
   #1594) are JSON-list KV values with add/remove UI; §2.5's `SettingSpec` has no list shape.
   Proposed: `value_type="list[int]"` etc. + kernel add/remove/clear workflows and a chip-list
   widget. Recurs across automod word lists, security allowlists, welcome roles.
3. **G-3 — `AnnouncementRouteSpec`.** "Event class → templated embed → bound destination channel"
   recurs (server-logging routes, welcome greetings, counters, spotlight). Declaring
   route+template+binding as data guts most of `server_logging.py`'s bespoke rendering.
4. **G-4 — `CommandSpec.cooldown`.** The shipped surface rate-limits at the command layer
   (`@commands.cooldown` — karma, economy, widely). The grammar has no slot, which would silently
   DROP shipped anti-abuse behavior at port time (a §5-class hazard, caught only because the
   manifest was written against real source).
5. **G-5 — declarative validator bounds.** Bounded-int/str-length validators recur (karma's two,
   welcome's, automod's); `bounds=(lo, hi)`/`max_len` fields kill a whole class of trivial
   tier-3 validator refs.
6. **G-6 — command-pool kind-scoping (§3.1 correction).** Prefix `!karma` and slash `/karma`
   legitimately coexist today; "commands and aliases in one shared pool" must be per-kind
   (Discord's namespaces are disjoint) or every dual-surface command is a false collision. The
   spike's own validator caught this on its first real manifest.

None of these weakens a freeze: G-1/G-3 make today-invisible wiring *declared*; G-4/G-5 preserve
shipped behavior the grammar would otherwise lose; G-6 matches Discord reality. Each entered the
prototype (`spec.py`) with a `SPIKE-FINDING` marker and is exercised by the example manifests +
tests.

### 2.4 Where the grammar strained but held (no change proposed)

- **Typed domain errors** (karma's five rejection shapes): absorbed by the §2.7 Result grammar —
  the handler returns `DECLINED` + `user_message`; the cog's try/except rendering dies. Good.
- **Game rules:** the grammar rightly refuses them; `renderer_override` + engine refs carried
  blackjack cleanly. The "worse programming language" failure mode (§10.1 risk 5) was NOT
  observed — no unit tempted a conditional into the manifest.
- **Static custom_id freezes:** `logging_panel.*` (8 ids) and `blackjack:solo:replay` declared
  verbatim via `custom_id_override` — and neither family appears in the spec's illustrative §3.1
  enumeration, which **re-confirms the spec's own rule that the generated inventory from the
  frozen repo, not any hand enumeration, is the authoritative population.** The harness capture
  independently verified blackjack's in-game buttons are discord.py auto-generated random hex —
  precisely what the versioned `g1:` scheme (§3.4) replaces.

## 4. Improvements to the rebuild approach itself

- **The port loop gains a mechanical step-zero.** `python3.10 -m parity.run capture --only <sub>`
  before porting a subsystem gives the band its red goldens in minutes; the sweep already names
  each subsystem's uncovered tail (the spec's required "coverage notes at flip" becomes a
  generated artifact, not an essay).
- **The spike's manifests are the port bands' head start:** karma/logging/blackjack arrive at
  Phase 4 with their declarations pre-written and source-cited.
- **A standing current-repo win regardless of the gate:** `parity.run check` is a behavioral
  regression net for risky `disbot/` refactors today (run it locally around a change the way
  `check_quality --full` is run).

## 5. The go/no-go readout

**Recommendation: GO — proceed to the Phase-3 K0–K10 build as specified, with the §3 table's
amendments folded into the design spec first (a half-day docs pass, no re-design).**

*(Schedule context: the same-day [parallel-execution plan](rebuild-parallel-execution-plan-2026-07-02.md)
models ~2 weeks active build with these two numbers as its throttles — both measured strong here, so its
center estimate stands rather than its stretch case.)*

The two linchpins now carry evidence instead of assertion:

1. **Harness:** built, deterministic (replay-proven), coverage measured with the tail named. The
   safety model has a floor number instead of a hope — 96% of the command surface replay-stable, with the thin deep-state dimensions (events 21%, tables 25%, settings mutations 2%) named as the port bands' curated-flow work. Coverage grows
   mechanically (more cases, click-path sweeps, persona variants) — the *architecture* question
   is retired.
2. **Grammar:** 73% as-written → **85% with amendments** on a deliberately hostile sample; the
   operator band (where the ~100-PR bulk lives) measures 97%. The "no hand-written views layer"
   thesis stands, with games correctly costed as the escape-hatch band the spec already priced in.

**What would have changed the verdict:** a harness that couldn't reach determinism (it did, in
one session), or an operator-band fit under ~60% (it measured 97% with amendments). Neither
occurred.

**Remaining honest risks for the gate call** (unchanged from spec §10.1, now with sharper
numbers): engine-bug blast radius (mitigations stand), importer correctness (unbuilt — Phase 4),
and the coverage ceiling itself: the number below is breadth on a live-registry denominator, and
the uncovered tail (COVERAGE.md) must keep shrinking through Phase 4 — every `pending → ported`
flip should raise it, never lower it.
