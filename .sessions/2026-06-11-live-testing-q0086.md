## What the session proved

- **The full AI model loop works outside production** — mention → policy →
  router → grounding → gateway → OpenAI/Anthropic → guard → reply, with the
  audit trail (`ai_decision_audit` provider/model columns) verifying every leg.
- **The layered guard design failed safe all day**: every model mistake
  surfaced as a refusal or an honestly-labeled answer, never a confident wrong
  number reaching the owner unflagged (the one that did — despos — was the
  tool-laundering hole, now gated).
- **The floor-model thesis (owner, Q-0095 addendum)**: after the routing/
  grounding fixes, gpt-4o-mini produced correct, subject-stable answers — the
  pipeline, not the model, was the bottleneck. Haiku 4.5 then served the
  Geraldo item list flawlessly on first grounding.

## 💡 Session idea (Q-0089)

**Triage-grade context on AI denials:** include a short, redacted excerpt of
the *user's question* (e.g. first 80 chars) in (a) the
`btd6_faithfulness: blocked` log line and (b) the `ai_decision_audit` row
(admin-only surface). Today the blocked-reply *names* are logged but never
the question — three separate times this session the agent could not map a
denial to its question without asking the owner for a screenshot, in a
session whose whole point was log-side diagnosis. Small, read-only,
self-serve triage; pairs with the bot-self-test-walker idea (its eval mode
needs the same join). (Dedup-checked `docs/ideas/`: gap-analysis's session
telemetry and AI spend metering are different lanes; no existing capture.)
