## 💡 Session idea (Q-0089)

**Automod shadow / dry-run mode** — a per-guild `automod_shadow` flag that makes the stage
*evaluate and emit `automod.rule_triggered`* (and optionally log to the mod channel) **without
deleting or warning**. An operator turns rules on in shadow, watches what *would* have been
actioned for a day, tunes thresholds against real traffic, then flips to enforcement with
confidence. Why I believe in it: automod's one real risk is false positives (over-blocking),
and the family plan's "off by default + exempt valve" only mitigates *after* a bad delete —
shadow mode mitigates *before* the first one. It's a small addition to the existing engine
(the verdict already exists; just gate the `_act` call), it's a well-known competitor feature
(Discord's native AutoMod has exactly this), and it makes the slot-5 logging service immediately
useful (shadow hits are the first interesting log events). Dedup-checked `docs/ideas/` +
`server-safety-and-automod-2026-06-12.md`: the exempt valve is covered, a shadow/preview mode
is not. Captured for automod v2 / the family plan's "later" column.
